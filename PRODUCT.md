# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indonesian manga/anime community members, primarily teens and young adults (16-25). They're active on TikTok, competitive by nature, and want a fun place to play mini-games, climb leaderboards, and connect with other fans. They use phones and laptops, often in casual settings: scrolling on the couch, competing with friends between classes, checking rankings during breaks.

## Product Purpose

MAE (Manga Anime Edits) is a community gaming hub where members play mini-games (quiz, riddles), earn scores, compete on monthly leaderboards, and participate in events. Admins manage content and community governance through a voting system. Success means members return daily to play, compete, and engage with the community.

## Brand Personality

**Fun, Competitive, Community.**

Voice is casual, energetic, and inclusive. Never corporate. Speaks like a friend who's also hyping you up for the next challenge. Achievements and rankings feel earned, not given. The interface should make you want to play one more round.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (blue/white, corporate card grids, boring tables)
- Overly childish gamification (cartoon badges, excessive animations)
- Dark-mode-for-the-sake-of-dark-mode crypto/neon aesthetics
- Cluttered gaming portals with flashing banners

## Design Principles

1. **Play first, navigate second.** The primary action (play game, check rank) should be reachable in one tap. Navigation supports play, not the other way around.
2. **Competition is visible.** Scores, ranks, and achievements are always present. You should feel the leaderboard breathing down your neck.
3. **Community surfaces identity.** Profiles, TikTok links, and admin presence make it clear this is a real community, not an anonymous game portal.
4. **Earned, not given.** Visual emphasis on achievements and scores that require effort. No participation trophies in the UI.
5. **One screen, one job.** Each page has a clear primary action. No dashboards with 12 widgets competing for attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Indonesian language throughout (primary audience)
- Mobile-first: most users will access via phone browser
- High contrast for readability (dark theme with light text)
- Keyboard navigable for desktop users
- No reliance on color alone for status (use icons + text)
